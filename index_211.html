Learning Objectives

  • Show how the CC scheduling rate increases the # of CC Appointments Scheduled, which is decreased by the CC appointment completion rate
    • Introducing stock and flow
  • Show how increasing overbooking increases completion rate, while also reducing time for CC tasks, which increases the no show rate over time
    • Demonstrates reinforcing feedback loop [Loop: overbooking reduces time for CC tasks and increases no shows] need for thinking holistically and in both short and long term to avoid unintended consequences
    • Demonstrates how structure can determine behavior: patient “no show” rates can be caused by the system (endogenous), not just a patient factor over which the team has no influence
